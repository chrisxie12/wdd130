Ghana

Kumasi is a city in the Ashanti Region of Ghana. It is the capital of the Ashanti Region and the second-largest city in Ghana after Accra. Kumasi is known for its rich cultural heritage, vibrant markets, and historical significance as the former capital of the Ashanti Empire. The city is home to several important landmarks, including the Manhyia Palace, which serves as the residence of the Asantehene (the king of the Ashanti people), and the Kumasi Fort and Military Museum. Kumasi is also famous for its traditional crafts, such as kente cloth weaving and wood carving, which are popular among tourists and locals alike.
